- **Relation to this repo:** direct follow-up to Runje & Shankaranarayana,
  *Constrained Monotonic Neural Networks*, ICML 2023 (arXiv:2205.11775) — the
  paper this package implements. Sartor et al. is the source of `mononet`'s
  **`mode="switch"`** layer parametrization.

## Why it matters for `mononet`

The ICML 2023 construction (`mode="absolute"`) enforces monotonicity with a
non-negative weight reparametrization `|W|` plus an a-priori split of each
layer's neurons across **three** activation classes (convex `ρ̆`, concave
`ρ̂`, saturated `ρ̃`) with hand-tuned counts `(s̆, ŝ, s̃)`. Sartor et al.
remove two pain points:

1. **No prior choice of activation alternation.** The "activation switch"
   makes a layer represent both convex and concave monotone pieces
   simultaneously, so the `(s̆, ŝ, s̃)` split is no longer a hyperparameter.
2. **Relaxed weight constraint → better optimization.** The switch is
   expressed over the *unconstrained* `W` (split into positive/negative
   parts inside the forward pass), which the paper argues is less sensitive
   to initialization than constraining weights to `|W|` directly.

Prescribed monotonicity *direction* is still available exactly as in 2023:
`|W|` gives non-decreasing; non-increasing inputs are handled by negating
those inputs (Remark 3.1); unconstrained inputs use free weights. So the
`MonotonicityMask` contract carries over unchanged.

## Core construction (Section 4)

Start from a single constrained layer `f(x) = σ(|W|x + b)`. Split the
unconstrained weight matrix into its parts:

```
W⁺ = max(W, 0)        (≥ 0)
W⁻ = min(W, 0)        (≤ 0,  i.e. the negative part kept NEGATIVE)
|W| = W⁺ − W⁻
```

so the affine map decomposes as (Eq. 11):

```
|W|x + b = W⁺x − W⁻x + b
```

Applying the nonlinearity to each part separately and **sharing the bias**
gives the two switch parametrizations:

```
post-activation switch  (Eq. 12):  f̂(x) = σ(W⁺x + b) − σ(W⁻x + b)
pre-activation switch   (Eq. 13):  f̂(x) = W⁺σ(x) + W⁻σ(−x) + b
```

Both are monotone **non-decreasing**: in Eq. 12 the first term has `W⁺ ≥ 0`
(non-decreasing) and the second is `−σ` of a `W⁻ ≤ 0` map (also
non-decreasing). In the linear regime (`σ = id`) Eq. 12 reduces to `|W|x` —
**not** an unconstrained map.

**Proposition 4.1.** Any function representable by an affine map with
non-negative weights followed by either `σ` or its point reflection
`σ'(x) = −σ(−x)` can also be represented by Eq. 12, up to a constant factor
(absorbed into the next layer's bias). *Proof sketch:* when all entries of
`W` share a sign, one term collapses to `±σ(b)` — `W ≥ 0` gives
`σ(|W|x+b) − σ(b)`, `W ≤ 0` gives `σ(b) − σ(−|W|x+b)`; recall
`−σ(−x) = σ'(x)`. Hence stacking ≥ 4 such blocks is a universal monotonic
approximator, and the switch is strictly more expressive than a plain weight
constraint (the latter is a special case).

> ⚠️ **Source inconsistency to be aware of.** Algorithm 1 is captioned
> "post-activation switch" but its printed body is the **pre-activation**
> formula (Eq. 13): `z⁺ := W⁺σ(x); z⁻ := W⁻σ(−x); ŷ := z⁺ + z⁻ + b`. The
> surrounding prose commits to Eq. 12 as the formulation used in all
> experiments (Fig. 6); the pre-activation algorithm/graph is said to live in
> Appendix A.5. **`mononet` implements Eq. 12 (post-activation).** FLOPs are
> near-identical (Eq. 12 evaluates `σ` over the `d'` outputs, Eq. 13 over the
> `d` inputs).
>
> Also note the prompt that kicked off this work wrote `W⁻ = max(0, −W)`
> (≥ 0) with the same `σ(W⁺x+b) − σ(W⁻x+b)`; that sign flip breaks
> monotonicity. The correct convention is the paper's `W⁻ = min(0, W)`.

## Theory (Section 3) — universal approximation beyond bounded activations

- **Eqs. 1–2** define monotone non-decreasing / non-increasing; the paper
  treats only non-decreasing WLOG (Remark 3.1: reverse by negating inputs).
- **Proposition 3.2.** Composition of monotone convex functions is monotone
  convex ⇒ non-negative weights + a convex activation (e.g. ReLU) can only
  represent **convex** monotone functions. This is the expressivity limit the
  three-activation 2023 construction worked around with bounded/saturated
  pieces.
- **Definition 3.3 / Prop 3.4.** `S⁺` = right-saturating, `S⁻` =
  left-saturating activations. Any non-negative-weight MLP with activation
  `σ` has an equivalent one with `aσ(x)+b`, so WLOG saturate to zero.
- **Theorem 3.5 (main).** An MLP `g_θ: ℝ^d → ℝ` with non-negative weights and
  **3 hidden layers** can interpolate any monotone non-decreasing function on
  any `n` points, provided activations are monotone non-decreasing and
  **alternate saturation sides**: either `(σ¹∈S⁻, σ²∈S⁺, σ³∈S⁻)` or
  `(σ¹∈S⁺, σ²∈S⁻, σ³∈S⁺)`. Proof builds half-space indicators (Lemma 3.6)
  and their intersections (Lemma 3.7) layer by layer.
- **Prop 3.8.** `σ` and its point reflection `σ'(x) = −σ(−x)` share
  monotonicity but saturate on opposite sides.
- **Prop 3.9.** ≥ 4 layers, non-negative weights, activations alternating
  between `σ` and `σ'` ⇒ universal monotonic approximator, provided `σ`
  saturates on at least one side. (4-layer bound matches Mikulincer &
  Reichman 2022 for threshold nets.)
- **Section 3.3 / Props 3.10–3.11.** *Non-positive* constrained weights + a
  saturating `σ` (e.g. ReLU) are *also* universal approximators — surprising,
  since non-*negative* weights + ReLU give only convex functions. An even
  number of non-positive layers is equivalent to non-negative layers with
  alternating `σ'/σ`.

## Experiments (Section 5, Table 1)

Method labelled **"Ours"** (post-activation switch, ReLU, default PyTorch
init, no extra tuning) matches or beats prior constrained-monotonic methods
across: COMPAS (acc), Blog Feedback (RMSE), Loan Defaulter (acc), AutoMPG
(MSE), Heart Disease (acc). Comparators include XGBoost, Certified (Liu et
al. 2020), Deep Lattice Networks (You et al. 2017), Min-Max (Daniels &
Velikova 2010), COMET (Sivaraman et al. 2020), Constrained MNN (Runje &
Shankaranarayana 2023), Expressive MNN (Nolte et al. 2023), Scalable MNN
(Kim & Lee 2024). Reported highlight: Heart Disease 94% ± 1%, AutoMPG MSE
7.34 ± 0.46. Practical note: the extra split-and-recombine "does not require
additional data transfers… we did not observe any overhead."

## Open questions the paper itself flags

- Whether **non-saturating** activations (e.g. Leaky-ReLU) can build monotone
  MLPs is left open.
- **Batch normalization** as an alternative remedy to the initialization
  sensitivity of the constrained case is noted but untested.
